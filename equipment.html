Equipment

This equipment is provided for each team at the start of the laboratory.  You do not have to use all of it!

Item

Description and Possible Uses

U-shaped magnet assembly

This will be a vital part of your motor, providing the magnetic field needed to make it work.

Perspex baseboard

A sheet of hard plastic, on which you can build your motor.

Polystyrene baseboard

A sheet of soft expanded polystyrene, on which you can build your motor.

DC power supply

This is built into the bench where you are working.  It provides the 3 V DC supply to run your motor.

Crocodile clips (2)

These allow you to connect the insulated wires with 4 mm plugs (see below) to parts of your motor.

Disc with reflective tape

You will attach this to the rotating part of your motor so that we can measure its speed of rotation using a laser beam.

Toolkit (5 tools)

Use the pliers for gripping and bending things.  Use the snips for cutting wire.

Materials

These materials are available on each bench.  You can use as much as you need (within limits) to build the different parts of your motor.  You do not have to use everything on the list!

Item

Description and Possible Uses

Insulated copper wire

Copper wire, coated with an insulating varnish.  Available in three diameters:  0.4 mm, 0.7 mm, 1.0 mm.  Use this to make the coil of your motor.

Paperclips - large

Bare steel paperclips, can be used as a structural element, bearing, electrical conductor, etc.  Maximum 4 in one motor.

Paperclips - small

As above, but smaller and lighter.  Maximum 4 in one motor.

Wooden blocks

Most useful as a former, around which to wind a coil of wire.  Can also be used as a structural element.  Maximum 2 in one motor.

Plastic tube

As above.  Maximum 1 in one motor.

Elastic bands

Use for holding things together, or as a spring.

Wooden rods (cocktail sticks)

Use as structural elements, where stiffness is required.  Could form the axle of the rotating coil.

Sandpaper

Use for removing the insulating varnish from copper wire.  See safety notes!

Adhesive tape

Use this to hold things together, or to attach things to a baseboard.

Putty (Blu-Tak)

Use small pieces to hold things together, or to attach things to the perspex baseboard.

Insulated connecting wires

These are fitted with 4 mm plugs on each end.  You can use them to connect the power supply to your motor.  You will find them hanging on a rack at the end of some of the benches.
